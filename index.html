What is big data?

Big data is the use of large data sets collected from users' social media usage, search queries, phone apps, and just about anything else connected to the internet. It is a massive industry and it is growing rapidly due to the constant demand for data. AI is used along with big data to create very detailed demographics about users and individuals.

Why does this happen?

Companies that collect large amounts of data often do it for three common reasons. The first is to improve their business by getting insight on what the consumers buy and prefer. The second is just to sell it. There are companies that specifically specialize in buying and selling large data sets to other companies for whatever purpose they need. The third and most well-known reason is advertising. Data provides demographics on users that allow ads to be targeted to them based on their interests and needs. These three reasons are what makes it such a profitable industry.

What are the privacy risks?

The main concerns of privacy regarding big data is security. When companies collect these large sets of data that can very often include personal or sensitive information about users, it poses the risk of security breaches from hackers. The laws about collecting and containing user's private information is currently quite vague and scarce. Laws take time to change and create, so data security laws have not quite caught up with the risks that exist today. Right now, the best way to avoid a security leak with your data is to avoid getting your data in those databases.
